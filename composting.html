Composting

In addition to recycling, composting is another environmentally-friendly method. Compost is organic material that can be added to soil to help plants grow. Instead of throwing out food scraps, consider putting them in a compost bin to keep them out of landfills and reduce the amount of methane emmitted in the atmosphere.


How do I compost?

You need three ingredients:

  • Browns: leaves, tree branches, and sticks/twigs. These materials provide carbon.
  • Greens: vegetable and fruit scraps, coffee grounds, and grass clippings. These materials provide nitrogen.
  • Water to break down the organic material.
  • You can also compost paper towels, pizza boxes, and paper plates.

After you have gathered your materials,

  • Place a bin (with its bottom cut out) near a dry, shady area next to a water source. However, if you cannot place your bin outside, you can also buy a special bin for indoors.
  • Place all of your materials in the bin.
  • Add water to the dry materials to moisten them.
  • Mix grass clippings and other greens into the pile. Make sure to bury your fruit and vegetable waste under 10 inches of compost material.
  • You may cover the top of the bin to keep everything moist.

Your compost will not be ready until about two months to two years. You will know it is ready when it becomes dark and rich in color. Once it is ready, add it to your plants!

See the graphics below for more information on what is and what is not compostable.


What Can Be Composted

From left to right, top to bottom: fruit and vegetable scraps, pizza boxes, leaves, egg shells, tea leaves, coffee filters, paper towels, egg cartons, toothpicks

What CANNOT Be Composted

From left to right, top to bottom: rice, meat, plastic cups, aluminum cans, glass, bread, dairy, clothing, citrus peels
