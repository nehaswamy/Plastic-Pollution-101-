What is plastic pollution?

Plastic pollution is characterized as "accumulation in the environment of synthetic plastic products to the point where they create problems for wildlife and their habitats as well as for human populations" (Encyclopedia Britannica).

In simple terms, plastic pollution = too much plastic = injured animals and humans.

But how exactly is plastic harmful?

  • Plastic in oceans kills millions of marine animals every years.
  • Some plastics, such as Polyvinyl chloride contain toxins. In fact, PVC is the most toxic plastic for the environment. Exposure to such toxins can lead to an increase risk in cancer, birth defects, a weaker immunity, and other health issues.
  • Most plastics cannot biodegrade, meaning that plastic products will last forever.
  • Toxic chemicals from plastic in landfills seep into groundwater flow into lakes and rivers.


Let's take a look at some of the numbers:

  • Approximately 150 million tons of plastic is only used once.
  • In 2014, the United States sold over 100 billion plastic bottles.
  • Many marine animals mistake plastic for food; approximately 1 million sea birds die from plastic, and 67% of the world's fish population suffer from ingesting plastic.
  • It costs $4,000 to recycle 1 tonne of plastic bags.
  • 6.4 million tonnes of plastic is thrown in the ocean each year.
  • The United States alone consumes 8.6 billion gallons of bottled water each year.
  • In 2015, the packaging industry produced 146 million tonnes of plastic and generated 141 million tonnes of plastic waste.
