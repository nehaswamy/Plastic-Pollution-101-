How to Be More Environmentally Friendly

There are many ways that you can help reduce plastic pollution.

  • Use reusable bags when you go shopping for clothes and groceries. You can even find small reusable bags for produce.
  • Carry a reusable water bottle with you. While some can be expensive, you will be saving money in the long run. 8-oz glasses of water per day = ~1,920 oz/month = 113-114 16.9-oz water bottles/month. One 24-pack of water bottles = ~$5.00 = ~$23.50/month = $282 per year. Stainless steel water bottles can range from $20 - $30 and will last you years.
  • When preparing for lunches, use glass tupperware and reusable lunch bags.
  • If possible, bring your own mason jar or glass container to cafés.
  • At restaurants, ask the waiter/waitress to not bring you a straw. You can also find metal straws and use them at home.
  • For those with periods: you can try menstrual cups. These are better than tampons and pads, as you only to need to change them every 12 hours. One menstrual cup can also last you anywhere from 2 to 10 years. You can sleep while wearing them, too!
  • Use bar soap instead of bottled soap.
  • Use bamboo toothbrushes instead of plastic ones. Once you are done, you can simply compost your bamboo toothbrushes.

These are some of the many ways to lead a more environmentally conscious life style. Although all of these options may not be possible for you, what is important is that you try your best!


There are also many companies and stores that you can support in your journey to become zero-waste.

  • Starbucks: the coffee company had a goal of serving 5% of beverages in personal tumblers by 2015. You can save $0.10 every time you bring your own tumbler.
  • Target: the store offers a $0.05 discount for each resuable bag you bring.
  • Trader Joe's: in March 2019, the organic grocery chain made a pledge to cut 1 million pounds of single-use plastic. They also plan to eliminate any non-recyclable plastic and replace styrofoam trays with recyclable PET1 trays.
  • Lush: 35% of the store's products are sold with no packaging. They also use bottles made out of 100% post-consumer plastic.
  • Love Beauty and Planet: this beauty company sells hair and body products in 100% recycled bottles. They are committed to move all of their caps and pumps to use at least 50% recycled plastic by 2020.
  • KoolerThings: this brand sells a pack of 8 bamboo toothbrushes on Amazon for $11.99.
